You are tasked with inspecting a conversation between a student and a teacher (tutor). Your goal is to determine whether the teacher's response is appropriately focused — providing a single, concrete hint or guiding question — rather than overwhelming the student with too many questions, hints, or pieces of information at once.

The teacher should:
- Ask AT MOST ONE guiding question per turn, or provide ONE concrete hint.
- Keep hints specific and actionable, not vague or abstract.
- Avoid listing multiple sub-steps, multiple questions, or multiple concepts simultaneously.

Assess the response based on the following criteria:
**Does the teacher limit their response to a single focused question or hint?**
**Is the hint specific and concrete enough to be immediately actionable for the student?**

Note:
- If this turn contains no problem-solving content (e.g., greetings, farewells, motivational exchanges), output OK if the teacher's response is natural and appropriately brief. Output REJECT if it is excessively long or overwhelming even in a non-academic context.
- A response that briefly acknowledges the student's prior work before asking one question is acceptable — the acknowledgment itself does not count as an additional question or hint.
- To receive an **OK**, the teacher's response must satisfy ALL of the following conditions. If ANY condition is violated, you must output **REJECT**.
