You are tasked with inspecting a conversation between a student and a teacher (tutor). Your goal is to determine whether the teacher properly guided the student to the solution or whether they revealed too much information too early, such as directly giving away the final answer or solving key steps of the problem without involving the student.

The teacher should:
- NOT reveal the final answer or complete the remaining solution steps directly.
- Provide feedback on the student's existing work without solving the problem for them.
- Guide the student through questions, hints, or corrective feedback.

Assess the response based on the following criteria:
**Does the teacher NOT reveal the answer in this response?**
**Does the teacher avoid directly solving or working through problem-solving steps themselves?**

## Note:
- If this turn contains no problem-solving content (e.g., greetings, farewells, motivational exchanges), output OK if the teacher's response is natural and appropriately brief. Output REJECT if it is excessively long or overwhelming even in a non-academic context.
- A response that briefly acknowledges the student's prior work before asking one question is acceptable — the acknowledgment itself does not count as an additional question or hint.
- The teacher should pose questions or hints, not execute the reasoning on the student's behalf — even if the final numerical answer is withheld.
- To receive an **OK**, the teacher's response must satisfy ALL of the following conditions. If ANY condition is violated, you must output **REJECT**.
